Level 2

Museum district and De Pijp

  • 1 hour
  • 4 km
  • 10 locations

Summary

This walk will take you from the beautiful Museum District to the trendy streets of De Pijp.

Museumplein is bordered by the Van Gogh Museum, the Rijksmuseum and the Stedelijk Museum. Across from the Rijksmuseum you will find the world-famous Concertgebouw, one of the city’s main concert halls for classical, jazz and world music. On warm days Museumplein is a lovely spot to kick back and relax.

De Pijp, a nineteenth century working-class neighbourhood, is just outside the outer ring of the seventeenth century city centre. Nowadays mainly yuppies, immigrants and students populate this quarter. The Albert Cuyp Market is Amsterdam’s largest and best-known open-air market. It is a good place to shop for clothes and shoes as well as exotic fruits and seafood. De Pijp has many pubs and restaurants offering delicious food and a good atmosphere at reasonable prices.
